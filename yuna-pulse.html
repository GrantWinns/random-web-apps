Key finding: Financial stress is emerging

Financial stress conversations surged 127% quarter-over-quarter, now representing 10% of all topics. This spike coincides with Q1 benefit cost increases and correlates strongly with anxiety (31% co-occurrence). We recommend considering a supplemental financial wellness resource for Q2. This is the type of population-level signal that traditional EAPs structurally cannot provide due to low utilization volume.

Engagement highlights

Engineering and HR remain the highest-engaging departments (47% and 52% respectively). Finance continues to lag at 20%, below the 25% org-wide target. New hires are engaging at 52% within their first 30 days — nearly double the company-wide rate — suggesting the onboarding touchpoint is highly effective. Weekend and after-hours usage accounts for 38% of all sessions, with peak engagement at 8 PM, confirming that 24/7 availability is a core driver of access.

Crisis response

Yuna triggered 12 escalation protocols in Q1, all resulting in successful handoffs. Two incidents involved emergency services (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline connections), both confirmed safe in follow-up. Average time to handoff was 3.8 minutes, a 12% improvement over Q4. A complete audit log is available in the Escalations tab.

Recommendations for Q2

1) Address financial stress with a supplemental resource — the signal is strong and growing. 2) Run a targeted engagement campaign for Finance department — utilization is 40% below the org average. 3) Continue leveraging the new-hire onboarding flow — it's the highest-converting activation channel. 4) Monitor the elevated-need cohort, which grew from 6.2% to 8% — still within normal range but worth watching.
